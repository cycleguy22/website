MyPubMap is your friendly guide to pubs and bars in Buckinghamshire.

Buckinghamshire (often abbreviated as Bucks) is a county in south east England. Milton Keynes (in the north-east) is the largest and most populus city; other notable settlements include Aylesbury (the county town, situated near the centre), as well as High Wycombe and Chesham (in the south). Bucks is one of the 'home counties', and historically had slightly different borders.

Currently we cover nearly three hundred pubs in the Bucks area - these are shown as red dots on the map below. Click on a dot to bring up a link to the pub's profile page. All the pubs featured on the site are also listed in our Directory section.



Currently we cover pubs associated with the following postal districts: Amersham, Aylesbury, Beaconsfield, Buckingham, Chesham, Great Missenden, High Wycombe, Marlow, Milton Keynes and Princes Risborough. Please note that whilst we have endevoured to include all pubs in the Bucks area, we are only human and some may have been missed. If you want your pub included, feel free to contact us and get on the map!
